Toldea April 5, 2018, 3:14pm #23

Oof, someone woke up on the wrong side of the Round Table this morning. I’ll try to respond to your thoughts.

What I agree with

I agree that the faction check day ability is meh. It’s intended to give a little social flavor to the class, and create interesting situations during a trial where the CW could viably lobby for (or against) the accused, but could also be easily lied about. Even under ideal circumstances, knowing with 100% certainty what a player’s class is doesn’t always save them / convict them.

I also agree that Magical Shield/conversion protection is a snooze ability. The intent was to give something for the CW to do between single use abilities, but likely it’s better just being out and having ONLY the single use skills.

Icibalus:

I don’t want to see a hybrid of all of those classes. It’s just needlessly complicated and swingy. Plus, it kinida takes away the weight of the special thing that only you can do.

I agree with this in part. I don’t think the night abilities are particularly complicated, but it creates actual choices for the player. It does somewhat lower the “specialness” of other classes abilities, but I believe that’s balanced by the single-use nature of the skills. As a bonus, it seems to fit the flavor of an actual “wizard.”

Icibalus:

So Sage is somehow even better than current Sage, who’s already one of the best conversions.

As I said in my post, I wasn’t wild about the Sage design. It definitely needs more work.

What I disagree with

First off, there is obviously some liberty being taken with the classification of “support.” Part of the problem CW faces right now is that a pure defensive class is already covered by physician, who protects against attack and poison. Magical Barrier protects against everything else, but is boring to play and generally has little impact (other than maybe frustrating a Mastermind for a turn).

Without additional mechanics to defend against, there’s not much room for a defensive support. So instead, I envisioned “support” as the CW filling the weaknesses left by increasingly dead BD roles. The CW “supports” the BD by filling in with single use abilities that mimic other role abilities. If the Dev’s want to disqualify me because I thought a little outside the box, that’s totally fine, I recognize that it’s not explicitly support. But this is another way the CW could go. And the whole point of the contest is to get new ideas, right?

So that’s my response to all of your “Try agains.” The abilities could have impact if used and timed correctly, which would make the new CW a high skill cap role.

Icibalus:

Killer ability, and literally a worse Cold Steel.

The whole idea is that it is a massive risk/reward, in order to restrict “yolo” attacks. As a kill ability, it shouldn’t be as good as the original.

Icibalus:

Why would you not target Mastermind. Because who needs Nightwatch, eh? I mean, it’s not like Nightwatch isn’t one of the weakest converts anyway cough cough

Even for your general level of sarcasm, you kicked it up a notch here and I can’t tell what your actual point is. MM’s Foresight is use limited, and depending on when the CW is converted the MM may be missing charges already. Even if he’s at full, it’s unlikely all 4 will be used before either the Assassin or CW dies/is outed. You would not target the Mastermind if your Assassin is under suspicion, or if he’s getting rando-blocked by that pesky Butler. It would also provide an avenue to get at the Drunk.

Icibalus:

Oh wow you made it even more busted than Frenzy. Good job.

This is likely my bad on the phrasing. When I say “Empower” I mean that the effects of the regular unlimited use Rune of Power apply (Occ Immune/Role check). Not “Empower” in the Frenzy sense.

Icibalus:

Because who needs reasonable kill power on a faction. Pfft? Balance. Nah, a potential 6 KPN in a 16 player game SEEMS LEGIT.

This is Circle of Death, but with poison. I’ve seen limited circumstances where CoD actually catches more than one person, but it’s usually a whiff. Being single use, and only poisoning, I think there’s value here. If you wanted to moderate it, just remove the kill for those already-poisoned.

Icibalus:

Limited use again and only serves to confirm the presence of a CW in the game.

Most class abilities confirm a role’s presence in the game: Hunter Wolf, Drunk Debauch, Knight Defense, etc. It’s all about how its presented socially to the rest of the group and whether BD trust the claim, or think it’s a coordinated evil lie.

Icibalus:

Overall: Even ignoring the ‘you can only have 2 night abilities’ this is a poorly balanced rework. ToL simply dosen’t need a Jack-Of-All-Trades.

I think given the limited mechanics remaining to defend against, and the clear direction that Magic Barrier is a boring ability, a Jack-of-All-Trades could be an interesting addition. I do appreciate the constructive criticism (even if I had to dig through some snark and sarcasm to get it).
