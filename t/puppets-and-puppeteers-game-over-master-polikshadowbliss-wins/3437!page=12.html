gingerbeardgentleman October 12, 2017, 4:09pm #232

All slaves perform their actions before anyone is enslaved or freed.

So person B would be made to attempt the enslavement of Person C, but he would also be freed that round (assuming no other actions against person B)

Person D would receive a message that he has lost person B as a slave, and may simultaneously receive a message that he has enslaved person C (depending on whether others also try to enslave C or C is a master, etc.).

and Person A would receive a message that person B is now free.

However, If a slave tries to free someone, they are told, “despite your efforts, (target) remains a slave”, regardless of whether or not their target actually is a slave.

Those are the only two results when someone attempts to free someone. This means that if somebody is enslaved on the same round that a different player attempts to free him, he’ll get the “despite your efforts…” message, and if someone already is free, someone else attempts to free him, and nobody attempts to enslave the target, the person attempting to free the target will receive the “(target) is now free” message.

In other words, the feedback from attempted liberations has nothing to do with the original state of the target, and only takes into consideration the final state.
