Alice:

I wanted to minimize alliance-building as people do this a lot on this site’s miscs.

I feel like the lack of the alliance building removes one of the core functions of how YTTD works and how it would work as a Misc tbh.

YTTD Spoilers below, beware:

Think about it in this way, in the actual YTTD game, Nao can convince Sara into voting with her, tying the votes, and killing everybody else. That element is completely removed and the Sacrifice is stranded to being exposed by their own votes, more than likely. To have someone ally with you in this game, you have to have someone you can trust. And based on what we know from Danganronpa Miscs, not many people are actually willing to kill to end the game early. The same would be said for here. Marl didn’t expose he was the Sacrifice when he won. He manipulated two other players to his WiM and only outed himself when he was exposed, but by then it was too late, Zone and Windward couldn’t do anything to stop him from being victorious.

In my game, I removed that element of “pick a person to win with you” and changed it with “pick a person to kill”, this was a horrible idea, I could’ve done so much more but because of the “First Strike, you’re safe, Second Strike, you’re out” type of rule, I ruined that sort of element that was possible. But because you’re now in a situation of “First strike and you’re out”, it’s a lot better of a decision to have a sort of alliance building system around the Sacrifice. If people have many allies, they wouldn’t want to betray them, which leads to a situation of where they might be exposed to knowing who the Sacrifice is, or many other things. In this one, the Sacrifice is always basically just a shot in the dark and just hoping not to hit them.
