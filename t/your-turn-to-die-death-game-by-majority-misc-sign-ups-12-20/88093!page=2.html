Alice June 12, 2021, 2:33pm #29

Eh, the problem here is that other people are doing it, tho. Plus, the last game didn’t really give people the motivation to avoid playing favorites as…

A) There was no risk in accidentally voting the Sacrifice as that role was made a joke.
B) There was no motivation for people to kill suspicious players because of how the MM worked.

Like, see how the games ran in the VN. Obvious spoilers below.

In Chapter I Joe, Sara, Sou, Kanna, and Kai were the candidates for being killed. Sara and Sou were basically thunderdomed as they were extremely suspicious of the motivation of the other party as there was a paranoia about traitors in the midst. Kai was there as his entire behavior with Nao was sketchy as hell. Kanna… well, she’s basically a liability because she’s extremely guillable.

After the second phase of the game they all decided on offing Kai as he was not only suspicious, but he was basically confirmed to be the Sage while there was a risk that they’d kill off the actual Keymaster in Sara/Sou or the Sacrifice in Joe/Kanna.

In Chapter II Nao, Sou, Kanna, and Keiji were the candidates as Sou and Kanna were as suspicious as they were in the last chapter and Keiji was sketchy as hell with how he handled Megumi’s victim video. Once Keiji was removed of the pool since Q-Taro vouched for him as the Sage then the game became trying to find out who was the Sacrifice in Sou/Kanna/Nao.

Wazza didn’t include these elements in her game as for the first part the MM could basically do nothing and was only a single person, so evils in that game so to say had effectively no presence and thus there was no need to kill off suspicious people. Then because the Sacrifice was pretty much neutered there really wasn’t a reason to actually hunt for them which meant that people could play favorites with impunity.

Again, the Death Game in YTTD can be played as an actual social deduction game. The only reason why it didn’t work out in Wazza’s game was because she effectively removed every danger that were present in the game.

I know I sound like a broken record, but way too people in private have told me that they don’t think that YTTD can actually be a viable social deduction game due to the previous run and as someone who’s an experienced designer of SDGs I wanna dispell this myth.
