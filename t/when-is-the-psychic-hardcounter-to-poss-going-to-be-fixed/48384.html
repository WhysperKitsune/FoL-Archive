Aquillion February 25, 2018, 11:32am #8

Yeah, I agree that a weak endgame is a much bigger problem for the psychic. But the psychic is part of it. Realistically, for a Possessor to win, you must have all of the following die before the last few nights:

  1. The King. He executes you 1v1, and you can’t get through his guards if he has any left. This is the biggest problem because the Possessor no innate way to kill a king who guards or who gets protected, and with starting kings starting with 6 guards and new ones having 4, with little else to spend their actions on, it is very common for there to be a guarding king every single night for the entire game. Possessors pretty much have to hope the king randomly dies when the population is too low to elect a new one, hope the king happens to have no guards at that point, or hope for a hail-mary and get elected themselves.

  2. The Prince. You can kill him, unlike the King, but it’s still near-guaranteed loss if you reach the endgame with him still alive.

  3. The Psychic. You can’t jump while he’s still alive without getting rapidly lynched, and not jumping before the endgame will get you killed by any competent court because you won’t be able to facelift to convince people the NK is dead and won’t have a credible claim that can pass a court looking for the NK.

  4. The majority of the Cult / Unseen. You can survive having one alive, but if two or more live to the endgame you are almost certainly screwed.

That’s a lot of things that have to line up perfectly for a Possessor to have a shot at victory. And this doesn’t even guarantee their win, it’s just the bare minimum they need to have any realistic hope at all. Even if all these people die, the remaining court can very easily get lucky and catch them - Butlers, Drunks, Princesses, Observers, and more all have ways of catching a Possessor, while Knights and Hunters can get you when you try to jump. Those are more “reasonable” dangers that any evil faces, but they’re on top of all the must-deal-with problems listed above.

For comparison, the Reaper pretty much bypasses all endgame problems through the combination of reaping + chill, plus they can empower-reap to get the king if necessary (and they can do it a turn earlier than everyone else because even if everyone realizes he’s reaped, there’s still a one-day delay before they get to replace him.)

While the Sorcerer, of course, doesn’t have to kill the other evils and can rely on their numbers to win the endgame.

So while the king is the major problem, the Psychic is still a contributing factor in that they add another layer to the long list of stuff that has to shake out perfectly for a Possessor victory.
