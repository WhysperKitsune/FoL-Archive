Aquillion February 22, 2018, 9:31am #23

Prince is intentionally more powerful than other BD roles because doing so makes the NK / evil victory dependent on how fast they find the Prince, which in turn rewards them for smart play. If every BD role were equal, choosing who to kill wouldn’t be nearly so important, and deduction wouldn’t matter as much for the evil side.

I also predict a certain meta: Prince outs themselves day 1, gets every Physician and Knight on them, and can then precede to basically become the King, since if they lie, the real Prince kills them.

The current meta seems to be much more biased towards the Prince trusting the King to protect them, for some reason - a side-effect of making the kings much more frequently good while also giving them 6 guards and nothing else to do (unless they have an identified evil to kill.) This is risky if the King is evil, but the benefits if the King is good are pretty high.

The Psychic is entirely capable of doing 90% of what the Prince does in the Prince meta anyway. They can’t execute or RB, but if you’re directing the entire court, those abilities aren’t as important. I think the real danger here is whisper games, not the Prince specifically; the Prince is just an obvious person to use for them because of his power and ease of confirming.

If we want to prevent it, though… perhaps something like the ToS blackmailer? Super-buff Distract? Give a whisper-listening day ability to the evils? (Maid Spy is ok, but the chance of them having it at any point is low.) Maybe even an evil ability that reads your entire whisper history, so evils can recover whispers retroactively - making whisper games more dangerous for the BD.

Alternatively, give evils more reliable access to a one-shot ability that auto-bypasses defenses.

(Part of the issue is that these tools theoretically exist, but practically the evils rarely have the converted person they need when they need them. Other than the general “people can fake their old roles”, convert abilities have almost no impact on the meta.)
