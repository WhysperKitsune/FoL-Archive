Hemogoblin March 29, 2018, 7:35pm #130

Day Ability [Cursed tongue] 3 uses: Works like a troll box but only evil and nk can see the message.

The point of this ability is to bait the evils into trying to convert/kill someone so you can save them with your night abilities. If you’re clever enough with the message you send you may make the evils out themselves by responding to it. For example you make 10 say “DO U KNO DE WAE” and then an unseen says “wtf that meme is old and unfunny” then get them exed.

Nigh ability [Barrier] works the same as current barrier.

Now you can make someone enticing to convert with cursed tongue so you’re more likely to protect someone. It’ll also feel a lot more awesome to trick the evil and then protect someone instead of randomly throwing a barrier out and it happens to protect someone.

Night ability [Explosive Rune] 2 uses: Target a player, if they die kill their attacker aswell.

I think a high impact ability that takes some skill to get value out of would really make CW feel more interesting. You don’t want to use this on a Prince or King because if someone else is protecting them and they don’t die your rune doesn’t kill the attacker. You could then used cursed tongue to make someone else a target and cast the rune on them if you think someone will kill them.

Current CW feels very passive and not very rewarding to play. Cursed Tongue and explosive rune would allow CWs to make plays that feel good when they pay off.

This is my CW rework.
Passive 1-Service-Immune to Occupation and Redirection
This would make it easier for NKs to claim CW and makes sense lore wise as the CW served the king and had to build his alcohol fortification.Also the CW has magic he doesn’t suffer from silly things like hangovers and he doesn’t get drunk.
Day Ability-Communicate-Talk to one dead player-unlimited uses-1 day cooldown
This would be just an ability to let CW talk to some important BD classes to find out info.Putting it on cooldown makes it so that you can’t do it every night so you might want to save it until you can.This would support BD as the CW would be the messenger from the dead to the living.This makes sense lore wise too because the CW has magic and can connect to the dead like the Psychic.or mystic now, could have.
Night Ability-Prophecy-Protect a player with a barrier that will block all negative effects.Occupation,mind warp,conversion,etc.Get 3 names and one of those names will be the one that did the effect.
Now you are not told what that effect was.It could be an MM or a butler.In addition it only works if a negative effect happened to that person the night you barriered.The reasoning behind this is your target doesn’t know if he was barriered or not.This also makes it an all right fake claim as long as the person you claim to be guarding was not occupied or had any other effects happen to them.This is of course a risk but there has to be some way to deduce who it is and this will separate the good liars from the greats.The other reason I want to implement this is to give CW something to do.For most of the game they just sit there and try to figure out stuff that is openly said.Now this ability would give CW some investigative abilities but not a lot.After all first your barrier has to do something.Second you need to try to get claims from the three people.After that you need to try and figure out who is lying if none of their logs help with that.And by the time you figure it out you might be dead or converted.So in general this ability wouldn’t do much except make CW more fun and supportive in some ways.If someone said I occupied 4 and you confirm that they were in your 3 names then you support their claim.
Night ability-Tornado-same old same old
These changes would make CW fun and supportive in my opinion.They also don’t allow too much confirmability as CW is pretty much the same but with a few changes which make CW harder to identify from the NKs.It also wouldn’t hurt sage too much as the only things different would be the barrier which is already different and the day ability which doesn’t work all too often.
