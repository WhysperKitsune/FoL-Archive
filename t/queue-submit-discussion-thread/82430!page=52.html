thepigeonnyc:

So, I came up with this idea, and idk if it would be FM or misc, but instead of there being an informed minority, there would just be two uninformed factions with about the same number of people. I don’t have any thoughts beyond that, but idk how to balance that. Does anyone else have any ideas?

Oh, interesting idea. I think you’d need to make this a non-vanilla misc with a few special roles to make this work.

I haven’t played the game yet, but you’ve probably heard of The Resistance: Avalon. Something interesting I read about that game is that the good/town side has one random player with the Merlin role. This player knows who all the bad/scum players are, but has to be careful to speak in riddles to their teammates so that the scum don’t know who they are and kill them off. So maybe each faction would have one Merlin role.

Additionally, you might have 1 or 2 other players on each team with special limited-use abilities to reveal the faction of other players. So some possible abilities might be the following:

  • Reveal the faction of a targeted player to yourself only
  • Reveal a random player of your same faction to yourself only
  • Reveal a player to everyone as their true faction
  • Reveal a player to everyone as their opposite faction

Another possibility with the 2 factions is a single neutral. Maybe the neutral knows the factions of everyone and can try to use this to manipulate everyone. Especially if there’s also the Merlin roles, they can try pretending to be one of the Merlins. Or maybe the neutral is a lone wolf, but they need to choose a faction at some point.

Another consideration is having 3 factions. So towards the beginning of the game, when you are voting for someone, instead of it being almost 50-50 that you hit a teammate, it’s only around a 1/3 chance. As the game progresses, you have to consider whether to work with one faction to try completely eliminating the other faction first, or keep things balanced.

Anyway, I think there are some possibilities for a game with that basic premise of two uninformed factions. Let me know if you are interested in discussing ideas further.
