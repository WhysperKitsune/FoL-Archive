You shiver as you search through the kitchen’s freezer, trying to find anything that could help you escape. But even after your efforts to carve dry ice into a key, you can’t manage to find anything that works. You head back to the main kitchen to regroup with the other eight people.

“Have any of you found anything?” you ask. The others shake their heads. A few of them hold up objects that, like the ones you were able to find, seem to be totally useless – a chunk of gouda, a whetstone, a bottle of oil…

Finally, your eyes alight on a young man sitting on a box in the corner, with his legs crossed fiddling with some object. “What are you doing?” you ask him.

“Trying to see if I can bend this spoon,” he says.

“Are you trying to make a key?” you ask.

“What?” he says. “No, I just thought it was interesting. Look, it’s nearly a circle!”

Just then, the door opens, and the strange rabbit-creature appears. “Looks like you’re out of time!” he says.

Your eyes snap to the timer, which has ticked down to zero. In your haste to confront your fellow shipmate, you hadn’t noticed your time slipping away.

“Well?” asks the rabbit. “Who’s it going to be?”

You shove the man you’re talking to at the rabbit. “He wasn’t trying to help us at all,” you say. “Take him!”

You hear the rest of your shipmates agree with you. The rabbit smiles. “Well, it’s not one of my spies, but I don’t particularly care,” he says. “This game of life and death accepts all sacrifices.”
