The Baron (Neutral Offensive) Original Thread

Details

Immune to Conversion

Passive: Royal Revenge - If you are executed for treason, the King will die.

Night Ability 1: Royal Decree (2 uses)
Take Royal Blood from them if they have it. Give Royal Blood to them if they don’t.

Night Ability 2: Fraudulent Shaming (Infinite uses)
If your target is voted for Treason, all the trial votes will be reversed. Execute will be Pardon and Pardon will be Execute. Abstains will be normal.

Day Ability 1: Proclaim Throne (1 use)
Reveal the King’s true alignment to you.
Note: Only usable when King is on Trial.

Day Ability 2: Prison Ordain (2 uses)
The Prince will not be able to execute tonight.

Goal: Survive to see the Blue Dragon perish.

Lore: “Adiart give me strength.”

The Baron was never always grumpy. He had seen dangerous times. Many people believed he had been at Adiart’s side during the war. Throughout the course of history, the Baron had commanded many armies, he had many victories on his belt, and held many accolades. It was no secret he held a grudge against the former King Adiart, because the seat of power had always been in his eyes. But he would never betray Adiart, loyalty like that does not waver. After Adiart’s passing, the new King that rose to the throne was naive and unpredictable. Most of the Blue Dragon would constantly contest the court and the Baron grew tiresome. He would make it his goal to end the Blue Dragon and form a new army of his own. Using the enemies of the Blue Dragon to his advantage, he could gain power.

You will cast a spell that forces your target to target somebody of your choosing (They will be unaware).

Lore: In the days of old, Wizards and Sages would battle to see who among them were the strongest. But they always discounted the Thaumaturge.

The Thaumaturge was the strongest of all those born with magic in their blood. They always stood firmly on their beliefs that Dragons once roamed the skies as Gods, while they also believed that the Blue Dragon were the reason they no longer exist. However, rumours flew through the magical guild that the Blue Dragon have an ancient dragon held captive below Castle Adiart. The Thaumaturge would forge an unholy alliance with Blue Dragon’s enemies, ensuring their fall to release the Dragon.

The Virtuoso (Neutral Support) - Original Thread

Details

Immune to Conversion

Passive: Musical Soul - If the music dies, you will die (Being Occupied will kill you).

Passive: Inmate Harmony - You will play a sweet harmony for the Prince when jailed, thus, allowing the music to keep playing as if you were not Occupied.

Day Ability 1: Passionate Composition (3 uses)
Your music will be loud enough to block out visits to yourself tonight.

Night Ability 1: Romantic Serenade (Infinite uses)
Sing a romantic song to somebody tonight, they will be immune to Occupation.

Night Ability 2: Royal Melody (2 uses)
You will play a sweet Melody to the court during the next day. All Royals will be immune to Day Abilities the next day.

Goal: Don’t let the music die.

Lore:

Here I am to tell you a story,
Of how I found such musical glory,

I found a little fiddle upon an old shelf,
Little did I know, it was carved by an elf,

Such fine craft work, with delicate sound,
I realized it wasn’t ordinary, what I just found,

Onward to the Palace to test my own skill,
But should the music stop, it is me they will kill.

-Written by the Virtuoso

The Virtuoso was a being of peace and harmony, often crying upon declarations of love and delivering lovely sounds of music that come from the passion in their heart. Within the Royal Family, the King wanted somebody who could keep him happy. He found The Virtuoso very humble and thus, employed him to play every day and every night, and threatened his life if he should stop. The Virtuoso didn’t stop, as an actual fact, they didn’t want to stop playing the fiddle.

Some nights the Prince would invite The Virtuoso into the cells just to hear the sweet sounds of The Virtuoso’s melodies. However, The Virtuoso was once a known drunk, and should they be tempted, wine was always going to meet their lips. That is what they had feared among the King’s staff. They feared that once the music stops playing, The King will have their head. The music must not stop playing.
