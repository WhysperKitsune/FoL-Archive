It is my belief that whenever you are confused, there is a clash between your beliefs/your assumptions and your observations, that is, something you believe/assumed is wrong.

however its not always immediately obvious what you did wrong. in fact, the obvious assumption can quite often be incorrect. for example, a magic trick. say a magician appears to pull two interlocked rings apart. you as the viewer are initially confused. your observation that the magician pulled 2 interlocked rings apart is clashing with your assumption that one cannot just pull 2 interlocked rings apart. the first thing you think, is that, obviously, the assumption that you cannot just pull 2 interlocked rings apart is wrong.
however, this is not right. in actuality, the assumption you made wrong was that the magician actually pulled the 2 rings apart.
