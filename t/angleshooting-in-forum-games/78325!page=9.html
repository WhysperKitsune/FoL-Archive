JakeTheWolfie September 23, 2019, 8:04pm #169

Essentially, the goal of moderation here is NOT to strictly define everything you can an cannot do.
The goal of moderation here is to make the game more enjoyable and more fair to all parties involved.
And that just so happens to also leak in stricter definitions. We don’t need strict rules, we need enjoyable and fun rules. We need rules that make the game more enjoyable for everyone involved.

However, despite this, moderation requires clear cut rules. It needs to make a gradient from black to white, and decide between which two nearly identical cases the line is drawn. However, you also have to consider different tiers of punishment. From warnings to permanent blacklists, there is a range of things to use for punishment. Going back to the 2 nearly identical cases, the one case which would be punished should at most get a warning. Now, this line between cases will always be fluctuating by the very nature of Moderation. But we still have tools to punish the almost right to the deviously wrong.

In short: Moderation brings strict definitions, but does not require it.
