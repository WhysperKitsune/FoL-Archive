Town Abilities

Town Vanilla - You have no abilities except for your voice and your vote. Use them wisely.

Town Even Night Doctor - On every even night, you may choose to heal a player, which will prevent them from dying if they are attacked.

Town Tracker - You may choose a player at night. You will see who they visit, if anyone.

Town 1-Shot Night Vigilante - One time at night, you may choose to target a player to kill.

Town 2-Shot Neighborizer - Each night, up to two times, you may add a single player to a neighborhood chat. This chat will be active only at night.

In this world of light and dark, the gods have blessed humanity with a gift. A gift of both creation… and destruction. Of all the empires and kingdoms that have stood, darkness has constantly laid breath at their borders, drawn to all the positive and negative emotions emitted from the walls.
Occasionally, there comes those who are willing to side with the Creatures of Grimm, those who wish for nothing but destruction. Beings who are far more evil than those monsters of darkness, for they were given the choice between good and evil… and willingly chose the latter.

Beacon was in utter turmoil. Grimm had started to overrun the Kingdom of Vale, and the Forest was constantly awash with the howls of the creatures.
A group of 17 students and faculty were huddled into a panic room. Noticing the rest of the occupants to be Beacon residents, Cinder signaled for Neo to cast physical illusions upon the team. Quickly, group of Cinder, Neo, Mercury, and Emerald were disguised as other residents of the kingdom. The disguises weren’t perfect, but they would hold… so long as they were not smashed.
It was surprising for the Academy to have been holding out so long. And they still hadn’t discovered the location of the Artifact hidden within. Cinder frowned pointedly. She would have to report this to #####. But… it didn’t look they would open the blast doors any time soon, as it required a majority of the occupants of the room to agree on doing so in order to open them up.
Well, Cinder smirked… A majority of living occupants. They wouldn’t be able to win in a head-on fight, but through subtle manipulation and trickery… It would be dangerous and difficult, with a decent risk of death. But it was the only way.
Cinder walked forward, casting the illusion of confidence. They would accept her voice, more likely than not. "Listen up! I think that someone in this room let those monsters here!”
This caused whispering to start. People began to throw suspicious glances around. Mercury followed her example. “Well, let’s be civil about this and use democracy to settle this.” Cinder chuckled to herself. Let the games… begin!
