If it’s really a problem then swap Offensive and Support for the Princess’s results and then buff the Princess with a day ability or something.

In any case the question you should be asking to determine type is not “what does this class do?” because all classes do more than one thing. But rather “what is this class’s function?”

For example while Butler does have a killing ability, his job isn’t to Kill but to hinder his target’s ability to act, thus he’s offensive.

Something to note is that Support is the inverse of offensive. In otherwords:

Heal target player = Support
Heal the player your target attacks = Offensive

This is because nearly all support effects (with the notable exception of some empower effects) work by preventing a negative effect. However preventing effects is also what offensive actions do. The difference is that Support actions prevent actions ON the target while Offensive actions prevent actions FROM the target.

This is why Tornado is Support but Debauchery is Offensive despite both of them having identical effects.
