Endgame flavor:

Amelia sat quietly at her desk. So many innocents had died – a doctor, a mage, and so many others – and it hadn’t actually helped anything. She thought back to the early days of the court’s proceedings, back when her greatest concern was her survival. When had she decided to betray the Blue Dragon?

Maybe it would be better to turn herself in. They wouldn’t be merciful to her, but perhaps they would at least be willing to spare her family.

“Scribe Amelia?” someone asked. She looked up to see King Zone standing by her desk.

“Your Majesty,” she said, straightening her posture slightly and shuffling her papers. “I didn’t realize you – what can I help you with?”

King Zone frowned. “This is likely my last day alive,” he said, handing her a sealed letter. “Read this tonight.” He paused for a moment. “I trust you to serve this kingdom well, Amelia.”

Amelia swallowed. “Thank you, Your Majesty,” she said.

The three remaining members of the court – the King in his long blue robe, the Prince with his axe, and an older Physician, with his medical satchel – stared at each other in silence. At last, Prince Italy spoke up.

“Shall we do the plan?”

Possessed nodded, holding out his wrists. “Chain me, your highness,” he said.

King Zone arose. “Very well,” he said. “Guards – let it be done.”

Two members of the King’s guard stepped forward. “As you command, Your Majesty,” said one.

“Long live the Unseen,” said the King. “May my sacrifice buy precious time for those of you who still exist outside the castle walls.”

Prince Italy gasped. “It was you?” he asked. “Then how did Marshal die?” He paused. “And how are you saying things that actually make sense?”

“The ways of the Unseen are mysterious,” said King Zone. “Guards?”

One of the guards took his sword and struck three times at the King. The King stumbled, then fell.

The Prince looked around at the remaining functionaries. “Let this be a lesson to those who would stand against the Blue Dragon,” he said. “Our enemies will pay.”

Amelia’s hands shook as she opened the letter.

Dear Amelia,

My associates told me of your sympathy for our cause. I regret that I was not able to do more to help us. It’s up to you now.

Zone_Q11

She picked up her candle, then, remembering how the Prince had ordered an increase in the number of guard patrols, set it back down. She couldn’t afford to be seen. She slipped out of her room and tiptoed down the hallways, until finally she found the corridor where Jgoes had said to meet her.

“Amelia?” whispered Jgoes’s voice from behind the statue. “Amelia!”

Amelia turned to look at them. They gestured for her to come over to where they were standing. Glancing around her, she made her way to where Jgoes was standing.

“There’s a passageway behind the tapestry,” said Jgoes. “We’ll be able to make it out.”

Amelia nodded and followed Jgoes down the passageway. They walked through the dark corridor for what felt like several minutes, before finally emerging into the outside world.

“So what now?” asked Amelia.

“Now it’s up to us,” said Jgoes. “We’re the ones carrying the legacy of the Unseen. We’re the ones with a chance to make things better for the kingdom.”

Amelia nodded. “At least we have a chance now. The Unseen might have failed, but we won’t. We’ll save this kingdom from the Blue Dragon, or die trying.”
