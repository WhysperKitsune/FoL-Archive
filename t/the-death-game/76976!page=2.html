I had allowed some conditions. I didn’t explain them in my game, but I will here, because it is up to Death to interpret the actions.

One, A movement can be made. Depending on the distance, the host has the power to let another anticipate the primary member’s action.

Two, after moving, or during moving, that must be SPECIFIED in your action. The host has the discretion to use logic on your actions, whether it is physically able to do as such, based on the host’s perceived condition of the player at that point in time.

“Magnus smacked James with uppercut”
And
“Magnus stepped forward, ducking his head under and twisting his body upwards, attempting to uppercut him.”

The latter will allow the host to understand more of what the character is trying to do. After all, I as a host LOVE to assume the worst.

Lastly, Writing Logic. If I say I chop off James legs and stomp on his dead body with my bare hands, that’s not going to fly. After all, a host has to ensure that outcomes are logical. An uppercut will cause the person hit to stagger backwards, and if critical, fall back, NOT FLY 10M away.

I also assumed accuracy is accurate unless something happens to said target, changing positions.
