Mechanics and Feedback

= Unique
= Guaranteed
= Magical
= Nobility

Unique and Guaranteed are the same as the normal kind that‚Äôs found on FoL.

There is no bleeding nor poison.

Magical is a characteristic that is mostly used for Deliverance members to find certain classes. Magical is tied to classes that are flavor-wise magic users in Fire Emblem.
Nobility is taken as a modified version from ToL 1.2.
All Nobility gain another secret vote when voting someone else as King, and will only be taken into effect or shown if it actually matters.

Arcanist‚Äôs first visit check is based on who people target first if they have the mounted target ability or multiple visits.
Kliff and Faye‚Äôs tracker and watcher result will give in the order that the plsyer inputted, so the first visit will always be the first person the target visited.

Nuibaba/Delthea has access to a neighborhood, where they can add and remove people. The people in that neighborhood will know who each other are, but Nuibaba/Delthea will talk through the host. The neighborhood can only be used at night.

Feedback is based on the limited version from EFOL 1.
Meaning: Players will not know if they are attacked and healed, just healed, empowered, mind warped, and missed, dodged, or survived Day Attacks. For non-Investigative classes, players will not know if they were occupied or redirected.

Basically means that most of the time, there will be a ‚Äúyou slept peacefully‚Äù or its easier variant, no feedback at all.

Exceptions:
Investigative classes will be given no feedback from their ability if occupied or visit prevented, and will only be told that they were redirected, not who they were redirected to.
Investigative classes will also get normal feedback from their abilities when not occupied or prevented.
All players will be told that they were killed, converted, changed classes, put in jail, and connected to the neighborhood.

Order of Mechanics:

  • Jail, Link
  • Visit Prevention
  • Redirection (Bypassing Immunity)
  • Occupation
  • Redirection
  • Mind Warp, Frame, Empowerment
  • Investigative Check
  • Guard, Death Immunity
  • Attacks
  • Heals
  • Conversions

Battles

Battles

Starting Day 2, at the start of each day, the Rigelian Army and the Deliverance will meet to engage in battle. The winning side will gain useful prizes each day that sometimes could swing the tide of the game.

This is based off of the classic ‚ÄúWeapon Triangle‚Äù from Fire Emblem, where Axes beat Lances, Lances beat Swords, and Swords beat Axes, much like Rock, Paper, Scissors.

How it works:

  1. Members of The Deliverance will give a claim of what they will choose for the battle the night before: either Swords, Axes, or Lances. This will be displayed in bold text alongside some descriptive narrative at the start of the day.
  2. The Deliverance will also give the real choice. There may be abilities that a class can use which allows them to choose Bows, forcing a tie the following day regardless of the Rigelians‚Äô choice.
  3. There will be 1 day in real time for all alive members to come to a vote in the thread by choosing Swords, Axes, or Lances. Some classes may have special abilities that affect this phase.
  4. The Deliverance will come to blows against the Rigelian Army and the choices of both sides will be revealed.

If Rigelians Win: Conversion Immunity that night (Can be bypassed by some abilities)
If Deliverance Wins: Choice of removing one cooldown from any ability
If Tie: Nothing happens

Day Attacks

Day Attacks

Starting Day 2, during the day, some classes will be able to directly attack their targets by using Day Abilities. Unlike night attacks, this will have a result based on luck. Different from most other Day Abilities, all Day Attacks can be used during Battles. Each different ability has different hit rates, and some classes have passives and abilities that can increase hit rate or decrease hit rate of various players.

The way notifications for Day Attacks work, is anyone who can use a Day Attack will message me the name of their ability in the DM. I will RNG it and see the result. The person who used the Day Attack will either get ‚ÄúIt wasn‚Äôt successful‚Äù or ‚ÄúYou killed them‚Äù the only way ‚ÄúYou killed them‚Äù shows up is if the target actually ends up dead. ‚ÄúIt wasn‚Äôt successful‚Äù can be due to a miss, a day attack prevention, or even an immunity. The only target Day Attacks may not go for is the King. Everyone else is fair game.
