Here’s a handy trick to check if a night action is social or not.

Investigative - The system is giving you feedback

Social - You are influencing someone else’s feedback

Note: Giving someone else the ability to do a social action on you is still social.

This means that gossip is social because it transfers feedback rather than creating it. And Drug is social because it gives someone else feedback. Yes that means that a sheriff that gave someone else his feedback would be social.

Alice May 31, 2018, 5:36pm #154

I always claim Obs as MM and it works 95%+ of the time as long as you pay notice to every little detail in the match and are well-versed in the game’s mechanics. You can base your follows off your teammates’ visits, the logs of dead players, and any little detail that comes up during the day, such as a Drunk claiming they were successful in redirecting a person the last night. Adjust your logs based on attempts at occupying you, add a prevented/occupied if you’re not feeling entirely safe with a night, and make sure you Peek only at late game. Peeks are the easiest as you’ll know all the players who are Unseen, and barring the Prince every BD-aligned player can be converted while every neutral cannot, so your Foresight will let you automatically know a player’s faction as long as they aren’t the Prince.

Assume the following game:

You are the Mastermind[6], and you know who is the Assassin[3] and you just converted a Duchess[13] the first night.
The Assassin[3] killed a Physician[9], who healed the Duchess[13] the previous night.

In the coming night the Duchess informs you that she visited Killer/Off[14] during the first night, so via that information and 9’s logs you put n1: Follow 13 - [9]>[13]>[14].

D3 comes the Assassin[3] dies to the NK and a Noble[4] is upped for claim as the court has reason to believe that he is the Possessor. The Butler[11] confirms him as not occupy immune as he visited the accused last night, thus he’s pardoned. You know that 11 visited 4 due to mutual confirmation from both and that since 4 was occupied then he did not visit anyone. So you put n2: Follow 4 - [11]>[4]>[None].

For N3 you want to convert the Butler to make up for the dead assassin, but he partied and occupied you, so since you know that he used a party you can easily put n3: Follow 11 - Occupied. You also noticed that the Sheriff[5]'s log has him visiting the Noble[4] n2, so you also add that to your second night.

Killer/Off[14] claims Hunter D4 and in N4 you attempt to use Foresight on him, and he cannot be converted. The Prince[8] died N3, so Killer/Off must be a neutral, likely the NK. So come D5 and you accuse him claiming N4: Peek 14 - Neutral.

By the end of this day, your log will look like:
Mastermind the Observer
N1: Follow 13 - [9]>[13]>[14]
N2: Follow 4 - [11,5]>[4]>[none]
N3: Follow 11 - Occupied/Prevented
N4: Peek 14 - Neutral
This makes the log look extremely convincing, especially as you’ve also found the NK, to the point that BD will likely not look too deep into it and will automatically assume that you’re either a legit Observer or at least a Nightwatch.

A game won’t look 100% like this, but as long as you pay attention to every single detail that’s going on it’ll be extremely easy to pull this off. The sheer amount of information that comes from Follow is a double-edged sword for BD as not only makes it difficult to fake unless you have an eye for detail, but if you’re good at faking it then BD will always assume them to be legitimate and not take any second glances at it. Hell, I’ve even pulled this shit when I was a Reaper and it worked far better than I thought.

As a side note, know who visits and who does not, as the Mystic, the Prince, the Hunter, the Inquisitor, and the Sorcerer at times do not count as visits. A player that was the first target of Puppet Strings will in addition visit the Puppet Strings’ second target along with their original visit. You can use this as a way to frame people and gives an excuse to cause mislynches as a Possessor, but I don’t recommend it as most of BD are absolutely ignorant of Poss mechanics and will end up executing you for all the wrong reasons.

Thing is, the odds of an unannounced visit messing up the logs is minimal.

Out of the 16 players, 1 is the King, 1 is the Prince, 1 is the NK, and 3 are Unseen ergo you will know who they visit. So by default at n1, at least 2 players do not have abilities that count as visits, 1 will kill anyone who they visit ergo you will know if they visited a person or not, and for three players you will know precisely who they visited. Usually one or two BD die the first night in Unseen games, so you will know who the dead people visit as they will have it in their logs.

Also remember that players that are occupied, jailed, prevented, or spying Nobles, Hunters, Mystics, Inquisitors, Princes, and Fools do not visit, so mathematically the number of players who will have unannounced visits is not that high, as to qualify for an unannounced visit then it would have to come from a visiting BD-aligned class that was not role-blocked, lived to the following day, and did not speak out and made their logs public the following day.

Claiming Obs is not a flawless strategy, but perfect fake claims such as Knights, Sheriffs, and Court Wizards will always bring suspicion onto you.
