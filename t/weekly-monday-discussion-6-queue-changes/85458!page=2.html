orangeandblack5 January 8, 2021, 2:31am #25

for example, the CDFM queue had 5 categories

FM, VFM, SFM, TFM, and XFM

FM would just be FoL here, as it was “official” games as part of the on season - in the absence of actual FoL games, this category could easily be ignored

XFM were “experimental” setups, reserved for small games in PMs or any time the mods wanted to try something out - think of it like the Experimental FoL games we had here. Thus, with a total lack of FoL nowadays, this category can also be ignored

The other three categories were described as follows:

VFM games are Vanilla FM games, with emphasis put on standard Town-vs-Mafia gameplay. VFM games will include only standard roles and no special mechanics and can be either open or semi-open setups.

SFM games are Special FM games, a category that basically contains everything that isn’t a VFM game. Any game with third-party roles, a closed setup, hidden mechanics, ITAs, or any other unique features should go here.

Lastly, TFM games are for either Tiny FM games (less than 9 players) or Turbo FM games (phases under 12 hours, but generally turbos have phases measured in minutes, such as 15/5). These should be separate as they don’t take nearly as much effort to play or host, so can be run much more often.

I think this breakdown makes a lot more sense than any of the proposed multi-queues, as it effectively guarantees that you can at any point find a game that aligns with your interests. I believe ToS FM also currently uses a similar breakdown, albeit they have Episodes for their standard FM queue. I’m not sure if they still use TFM or retired the category, but it never encapsulated turbos there even thought that makes a lot of sense to me. They did also have GFM for big games (and I mean really big games), but we could always take this category for games larger than 17p if we want to allow more density of SFM games by allowing larger ones to run in a separate queue.

I just think sorting by game type makes way more sense than sorting purely by playercount, and even Idea 3 interlinks those two things in a way I don’t think is particularly well defined. I’d be down to do Idea 3 with games larger than 17p automatically relegated to SFM instead of VFM, but I do think having games with really small playercounts run in a different queue also makes sense rather than having them hold up 9-17p games.
