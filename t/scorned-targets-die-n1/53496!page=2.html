Aquillion March 22, 2018, 5:20am #32
Solic:

This isn’t a question about balance for me, this is a question about new user friendliness. Adding more complexity to an already complex role is detrimental for the new player experience. Scorned already has arbitrary rules like having only BD targets, except Hunter. I don’t think she requires more for an extremely fringe case. Probabilistically it is extremely unlikely that exactly the 2 scorned targets die n1.

I considered that, but honestly, this isn’t complexity that matters. Let’s say we change it so “if both your targets die N1, you get two new targets.”

Aside from occasionally keeping the Scorned from randomly vanishing D1, does this actually affect anything else? That is, are there any situations at all where anyone, ever, would need to know this rule? Usually I’m against unwritten rules, but in this case I’m not sure why it would even need to be written down - realistically I can’t picture a single situation where anyone’s gameplay would be informed by it; it doesn’t affect strategy at all, it just prevents a random edge-case.

Realistically… nobody but the Scorned is going to know their targets N1. And the Scorned isn’t ever going to be well-advised to do anything that would identify them N1 (the chance of this happening is too low to justify playing around it.) So it doesn’t affect gameplay beyond removing a bit of unlikely randomness.

Basically, complexity is only increased by a rule if people have to think about it. Nobody, as far as I can tell, would ever have to think about an N1 Scorned target replacement clause. If it were N2 or N3, sure, the Scorned would have to think about it because it might affect their decision on what to do D2 or D3 - that’s why I suggested that it just be N1.

But I think an N1 replacement is “safe” in that it doesn’t impact anything outside of removing an unlikely but undesirable edge-case.

Solic:

I agree with your point, however I just think randomly restoring the scorned’s objective is too much of a convenient cop-out for the Scorned.

Why, though? The Scorned can’t realistically do anything to influence what happens N1, not without basically ruining the rest of their game (and it’s completely unrealistic to expect Scorned to do this every time, so it’s still just random.)

Beyond that, why do you care about whether it’s a “convenient cop-out for the Scorned?” Seriously, this is something I see in a lot of suggestions, and it annoys me - this sense of vague, aimless “fairness”. Focus on the gameplay, not on what’s “fair”. Here’s the situation and the options as I see them.

The Scorned’s targets die N1. This is essentially random and there’s no reasonable way to influence it. The options are therefore:

  1. The Scorned gets new targets. This silently corrects the issue with no further impact on the game.

  2. The Scorned dies / leaves. This removes the Scorned as a factor, totally at random.

It’s obvious which we should go with.
