[Class Suggestion] The Ronin

Class/Lore/Fanfic
ThePlex June 30, 2017, 1:01am #1

#The Ronin (Neutral Wildcard)
Immune to Conversion

Passive Ability: Bushido
If you choose not to use your abilities tonight, you will meditate and recover a usage of a random night ability

Passive Ability: Shinto
The Gods will reveal to you how somebody was killed previously at the beginning of each night.

Night Ability 1: Nukiuchi (2 uses)
Draw your blade and duel your target tonight. You will kill your target but because you have respect those of Royality, you will not harm those of Royal Blood. (Still counted as using ability)

Night Ability 2: Kyudo (2 uses)
Fire your bow and injure your target tonight. If they had immunity, they lose it the following night. If they had no immunity, they will have to recover and will not be able to speak during the day.

Day Ability 1: Zen (1 use)
Meditate during the day. You won’t be able to speak but you will be immune to day abilities.

Goal: Survive to the end.

Lore:
“Without my Master, I am alone. Now, I seek a path of redemption, through this chaotic time.”

The Ronin, a master-less samurai from the Eastern lands. Came all the way to Adiart to seek redemption for his fallen Master. Using various teachings from the Samurai way, he must learn to survive and adapt in a war-torn environment. Only then can he return to the Eastern lands.

There are rumours that he communicates with the Gods to learn of the fate of others around him. Among the rumours are that he possesses a mighty blade in which he uses for duels. He also carries with him a strong oak bow, and with him, Kyudo, Way of the Bow, ancient teachings from the Samurai.

Many Samurai studied Bushido when training the Way of the Warrior. With this, he would be permitted to carry out more challenges with his blade and his bow. Aside his tough training and his ancient ways, he can very peaceful. He sits silently in Zen and nobody would bother him in fear of his rage.

Not being used as a lore for the class but I want to give a small bit of info on ‘Ronin’ warriors of Japan:

Because the former samurai could not legally take up a new trade, or because of pride were loath to do so, many rōnin looked for other ways to make a living with their swords. Those rōnin who desired steady, legal employment became mercenaries that guarded trade caravans, or bodyguards for wealthy merchants. Many other rōnin became criminals, operating as bandits and highwaymen, or joining organized crime in towns and cities. Rōnin were known to operate, or serve as hired muscle for, gangs that ran gambling rings, brothels, protection rackets, and other similar activities. Many were petty thieves and muggers. The criminal segment gave the rōnin of the Edo period a persistent reputation of disgrace, with the image of thugs, bullies, cutthroats, and wandering vagrants.
