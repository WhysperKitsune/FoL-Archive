ShadowKnight July 8, 2018, 2:42pm #27

The existence of a hardcounter adds a level of tension to gameplay that prevents optimal and predictable decision-making and, from a design standpoint, allows you to add more power to the other class.

The Deathcaller is as powerful as it is because the Shaman is breathing down its neck. Without a hardcounter limiting optimal decisionmaking, the Deathcaller’s play pattern becomes predictable and refined for maximum value and needs to be toned down so that the win rate isn’t too high.

Hardcounters prevent the main class from feeling overly oppressive to others, prevents the player from feeling like there’s no tension to the class and allows the player of the hardcounter to experience a completely different playstyle from normal, which adds to the overall variety of experiences in the game.
