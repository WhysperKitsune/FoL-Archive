Gameplay

  • The set-up is a 13 player set-up with 8 Vanilla Townies, 2 different Town Jacks-of-All-Trades, 1 Mafia Jack-of-All Trades, and 2 Mafia Goons.

  • Each JoaT has access to three abilities. Town JoaTs may only use each ability once. The mafia JoaT must use each ability once before using the same ability twice; however, it is not required to use them in the same order each time.

  • The Mafia JoaT may perform the factional kill and use a JoaT ability in the same night

  • Days are 48 hours unless majority is reached early and nights are 24 hours.

  • Executions are majority and plurality. Ties will be determined by RNG among all tied players.

  • No-execute is a valid vote, and may be hammered to end the day early. In cases where no-execute and another vote are tied at the end of the day, no-execute will have priority in the rand.

  • Self-votes are considered valid votes.

  • Votes are locked in LyLo (execute-or-lose) scenarios, but not in MyLo (misexecute-and-lose) scenarios. That is to say, in situations where town must execute correctly or lose, the first vote cast by a given player is final and cannot be changed.

  • All votes must be in the form /vote [name of player] to be considered valid, and all unvotes must be in the form /unvote. This is to enable the smooth processing of votes.

  • The Mafia factional kill is assigned. If the member performing the factional kill is not specified, the last member to submit a kill will be assumed to be performing it.

  • The Mafia factional kill is mandatory. If no factional kill is submitted, the kill will be determined randomly from among living members of the town.

  • The Mafia will communicate through a private Discord server or forum topic.

Flavor

  • By popular request, the game will be lightly Danganronpa flavored. I will try to avoid spoilers, but there may be some minor ones.
  • Flavor is neither role nor alignment indicative.

Pregame flavor

Hope’s Peak Academy. Ever since you were little, you’ve been hearing about Hope’s Peak – from your teachers, saying that if you worked hard enough you could one day attend; from the media, saying that Hope’s Peak was one of the most prestigious schools in the nation, that a Hope’s Peak diploma guaranteed success; from your parents, saying that while of course they loved you no matter what, they would be a little disappointed if you didn’t get in. Still, eventually you were scouted by Hope’s Peak, and today was supposed to be the day you finally started attending.

But the moment you walked in the door, something changed. Your vision began to blur, and you stumbled forward, then fell. The next thing you knew, you were waking up in an empty classroom…

A television in the classroom crackled to life, and on the screen a giant stuffed bear appeared. “Good morning, Hope’s Peak Academy!” said the bear. “Please meet in the gym in five minutes.”

Still slightly unsteady, you clambered to your feet and made your way down the hallway to the gym. Other students trickled in, one by one, until eventually there were thirteen of you in the gym. You frowned for a moment – weren’t classes at Hope’s Peak normally larger? – but then a giant bear jumped on stage, jolting you from your contemplation.

“Well, I’m just going to cut riiiight to the chase!” said the bear. “All of you are going to participate in a Killing Game!!!”

“A killing game?” asked someone next to you.

“Thaaat’s right!” said the bear. “I’ve recruited three of you to work with me, and each night, they’ll be allowed to kill one of you! Of course, each day, you can choose a person to be PUNISHED! If you can find and punish all of them, then everyone who’s still alive gets to graduate. But if you can’t, then the killers get to graduate, and everyone else will be punished!!”

You glanced back and forth between the people around them. Three of them were plotting to kill you. It felt unbelievable, like something from a game. But this wasn’t a game. It wasn’t a story. It was your new reality.
