What Do You Mean “Other Ways To Vote?” (WDYM"OWTV")

first, announce that this specific modifier is in play, and link players to this post so they can understand how it works
Votes for the lynch will now be calculated as follows. nonstandard methods contain links to demonstrations:
abilties modifying majority for the day have no effect
abilities modifying the number of votes needed for a given player to be lynched have no effect.
gladiating abilties choose their amount of candidates for the day, and move straight into phase 3.
vote doublers or increasers only affect an individual during phase 3
If no-lynch is a valid target, no-voters should be treated as a vote for no-lynch (0, all 1 but no-lynch 5, no-lynch wins each 1v1 or unapplicable) for each phase
if no-lynch is not a valid target, no-voters should be treated as if their vote slot does not exist for that round, and votes are otherwise counted normally.
anything else: WotM
phase 1:
for the first half of the voting period, players submit a range for the amount of people they’d like to be up to lynch, inclusive (they could submit “3” for a total of 3 wagons, or 2-4 to show they are voting for 2 wagons, 3 wagons and 4 wagons) the number with the highest total votes is the number of available wagons in phase 3.
in other words, the number of candidates is chosen by an approval vote. basically plurality
(from here on out when i say ‘n’ i mean the number of candidates as decided by this phase)

phase 2:
still within the first half of the voting period (players can submit for both phase 1 and 2 simultaneously), players assign each player a score from 1 to 5 (decimals are allowed) 5 is most want to lynch, 1 is least. average each players score, and the highest n scores move on as candidates to phase 3
in other words, the candidates for lynch are chosen via a score vote

Phase 3:
for the rest of the voting phase, the lynch will be determined via the Condorcet method. voters vote for every 1v1 match-up from the available candidates (if there are 3, players vote on AvsB, AvsC, and BvsC), to determine who they would rather lynch out of those two. given a tie, no one is lynched
a relatively standard Condorcet vote

the main reasons for phases 1 and 2 is to limit the candidates during phase 3, so players dont have to decide over 120 matches in a standard 16 player game on day 1
