This is the thread for awards nominations! Please be sure to include (1) who you are nominating, (2) what award you are nominating them for, and (3) a short description of why you are nominating them.

Please ensure that all discussion is strictly on-topic.

Credit to the Mafia Universe mods for creating the system that I’ve modelled these awards off of.

Rules

  • Anyone who has played at least one game on FoL can nominate another member for an award. Anyone who has played or hosted at least one game on FoL is eligible to receive an award.
  • All nominations must have a nomination and a second to be valid. You cannot nominate yourself, nor can you nominate a team that you were part of (for example, if you were scum in FoL 99, you can’t nominate the FoL 99 scumteam for a group award; however, you could nominate a teammate for an individual award).
  • A minimum of 3 days must pass between a game ending and any nominations for that game.
  • All games that occurred at least in part in 2021 are eligible for awards. For this year only, there is also a “legacy” category, for games prior to 2021.
  • The deadline for nominations is one week after the last game that began in 2021 ends.
  • Once the nominations period is over, all relevant awards will be eligible for voting. Voting will be done via forum poll (the results will not be visible to anyone except forum moderators).

Awards

Individual Awards

Summary

Best Town Perfomance - Single Player (small game): For a good individual performance as town in a game with 15 or fewer players.

Best Town Performance - Single Player (large game): For a good individual performance as town in a game with 16 or more players.

Best Scum Perfomance - Single Player (small game): For a good individual performance as scum in a game with 15 or fewer players.

Best Scum Performance - Single Player (large game): For a good individual performance as scum in a game with 16 or more players.

Best Neutral/Third Party Performance: For a good individual performance as a neutral role, in a game of any size.

Best Misc Performance: For good performance in a Misc game. In extraordinary circumstances, a team may be nominated for this award.

Best Use of a Power Role: For a player who displayed skill in using their Power Role (PR). This may be any PR, even a very weak one.

Best Mechanical/Strategical Player: For a player who consistently displays good knowledge of mechanics and uses them effectively, as either alignment.

Best Replacement Player: For a player who consistently replaces into games, and plays solidly after doing so.

Rookie-of-the-Year: For a player whose first game was in 2021.

Most Improved Player: For a player whose first game was PRIOR to 2021, but who improved significantly in 2021.

Most Dedicated/Enjoyable/Enthusiastic Player: For a player who is dedicated, enthusiastic, and enjoyable to play with, even when the game is going poorly.

Best Team Player: For a player who does a good job at working with their teammates, regardless of their alignment.

Best Misc Player: For a player who consistently does well in Misc games.

Best Town Player: For the best overall town player.

Best Scum Player: For the best overall scum player.

Best All-Around Player: For a player who is “good at everything.”

Team Awards

Summary

Best Town Performance, Group (Small Game): For collective excellence as town, in a game with 15 or fewer players.

Best Town Performance, Group (Large Game): For collective excellence as town, in a game with 16 or more players.

Best Scum Performance, Group (Small Game): For collective excellence as scum, in a game with 15 or fewer players.

Best Scum Performance, Group (Large Game): For collective excellence as scum, in a game with 16 or more players.

Hosting Awards

Summary

Best Host: For a host who consistently hosts well-designed and enjoyable games.

Best Flavor: For a host who writes good flavor.

Best Setup: For an interesting, enjoyable, and well-designed setup.

Best Misc: For an interesting, enjoyable, and well-designed Misc.

Best Role/Mechanic: For a role or mechanic that was both interesting and well-designed.

Fun Awards

Summary

Funniest Moment: For the funniest moment.

Best Fakeclaim: For the most effective fakeclaim.

Community MVP: For the community member most willing to help out the community and make it a good place to be. Mods are not eligible.

Legacy Awards

Summary

Best Town Performance (Individual): For the best individual performance as town, in a game (of any size) prior to 2021.

Best Scum/Neutral Performance (Individual): For the best individual performance as scum or neutral, in a game (of any size) prior to 2021.

Best Town Performance (Group): For the best collective performance as town, in a game (of any size) prior to 2021.

Best Scum Performance (Group): For the best collective performance as scum, in a game (of any size) prior to 2021.

Best Setup: For an interesting, enjoyable, and well-designed setup, prior to 2021.

Best Misc: For an interesting, enjoyable, and well-designed Misc, prior to 2021.

I nominate…

Marluxion/Chloe, and Maple/Ephemeral for Best Scum Performance, Group (Large Game) in Morphogenetic Mafia Thread A/Thread B

This game was a bit weird mechanically, given the “hydras, but not really, but also yes” nature of it, as there were two threads with 9 players each, and each slot had one player in each thread.

Both mafia slots properly utilized this to dominate thread control in the first thread, which caused a chokehold over the entire game. In the second thread, while they weren’t the dominant voices themselves, the wolves were strongly townread by every dominant voice, allowing them to still control the general narrative. This is all the more impressive when you consider that the second thread had multiple traditionally strong villagers.

Additionally, the mechanical play of the wolf team was extremely good – targetting a PR for nightkills every single night, and, despite a wolf being Jailkept N1, they never lost their cool, and continued doing their thing.

Both slots played exceptionally well in all these regards, and as a team. This is most evident if you read through the team chat, where there are literally thousands of messages between them. All four of them were bouncing off each other, both strategically and morally.

@Chloe for Most Dedicated/Enjoyable/Enthusiastic Player, and Best All-Around Player

“Dedicated, Enjoyable, Enthusiastic” are three words I think define Chloe extremely well, both inside and outside of the game.

Chloe has shown in every single game she’s played that she is not there to give it anything less than 100%. She will post hundreds, sometimes thousands of times… and these aren’t just one liners. While doing this, Chloe manages to consistently be the most enjoyable player in the game. Time and time again, she will be a shining beacon of good vibes. She is the life of the party, and brings it with her wherever she goes… while she herself, is also partying hard.

Now, while being all of these things, Chloe is very competent as both alignments. As a villager, she is able to consistently be found as one, letting her commonly become a town leader, which she is very capable of doing. Her WiM is consistently extremely high, and she is able to view the game from multiple angles, including some unique perspectives, which commonly allows her to have greater than average reads in these games.

As a wolf, Chloe has displayed the ability to shake off any struggles in her way and translate them into a scarily strong social game – and has proven to be capable of turning her strengths as a villager into a surprisingly capable wolf game.

All of these traits together just turn Chloe into a player who you want to play with, and you’ll enjoy it so much you might forget that you should be scared to play against her.

@Arete for Best Flavor

Whenever Arete is writing flavor for a game, you can consistently guarantee that the flavor will be worth reading. Whether it be good storytelling, or just amusing – commonly both – time after time, they’re able to consistently deliver on this front, always providing a high quality of flavor at the very least.

@eevee for Best Legacy Scum/Neutral Performance (Individual) in Enemy Within 4

Watching eevee play this game didn’t really feel fair. He was multiple steps ahead of the town at all times, not even using his converts until N5 and N8; because he simply didn’t need to use that.

Their mechanical play was incredible as well; protecting themselves from a vig shot N1, then preventing themselves from being cop checked later on, there’s a level of play that I don’t think very many people can replicate here.

Eevee was just playing his own game, and the rest of the players were his pawns, until it was too late.

@Wazza for Best Legacy Scum/Neutral Performance (Individual) in Rokugan FM

Was her role a bit overtuned? Yes, it absolutely was.

Does that discount how well she played in that game? No, I don’t think it does.

Wazza being able to convince half of the game to claim to some extent, via several different manipulative actions – all on D1 – is not a small order, especially with multiple people explicitly trying to stop her. also lying darkness funny

I’d like to nominate @min for Best Fakeclaim in Henry Stickmin Vig10er

Playing in a vanilla game with a single PR won’t lead to ‘fancy’ or outside-of-the-box plays, so while this action wasn’t anything particularly crazy, it still played a huge role in the outcome of the game; that being a town win.
In Henry Stickmin Vig10er, after noticing that the n1 shot was on somebody in their PoE, and somebody that not many players would have shot, min took the opportunity to claim vigilante and cover for the real one (myself), which served to remove themself from the PoE, absorb a nightkill, and also put the true vigilante in a position where they could make reads with knowledge that the wolves were fooled, and thus would be pushing agenda towards them.
