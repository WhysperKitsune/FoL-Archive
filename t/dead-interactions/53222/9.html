orangeandblack5:

However, the fact that, in ToL, you don’t know who your teammates are, or how big it is, definitely hamstrings this aspect, and turns dead interaction into a more negative force overall.

Unseen and Cult already know their allies.

But even if you are BD you know you are telling an ally information. And if you are reanimated, you need to judge who to use the ability on.

It definitely doesn’t work against their own team.

I also feel that these 3 of Aquillon’s arguments are strong reasons for keeping dead interaction.

Quote
Don't remove interactions with the dead. Add more interactions with the dead

The ability for the dead to talk to the living smooths out randomness a little bit by ensuring that it’s still possible to get their results on their last day (so, an investigative dying on the same night that they visit someone doesn’t always mean their results are lost forever.)

Communication with the dead leads to more strategically-deep gameplay because it adds more people who can devote their time to analyzing events and generally applying social deduction, then gives them a way to relay that to the living.

Fun is an important part of the game (it is, after all, a game.) Yes, the game needs to be balanced to be fun, but “Call The Dead is fun” and “it’s fun to hang around as a dead and try to give good advice to the mystic” are totally good arguments for keeping the ability.
