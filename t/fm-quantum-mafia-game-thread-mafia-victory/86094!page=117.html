Reasoning

Yes.
That is the underlining principle of statistics; that something with a higher chance of probability of occurring thus happens more often.

I get your POV; you may not trust those statistics, considering us following a 5% increase is counter-intuitive, an early-game mess when we should be prepping for late game. By the time our roles all settle, though, it takes only one clever individual to work out the one universe we’re in, because it’s the only possible world we could be living in. Thus, late game is not an issue, regardless of whether it’s easy for us to read others or not.

Of course, there never are ironclad guarantees around prediction, which is what Quantum Physics is about; the future can have more possibilities than we ever imagined. It’s important to know that statistics is not propaganda, even though I don’t believe that’s what you were getting at. Can you confirm this when you read it?

Last point for now; you’re saying that a 5% increase of odds don’t matter significantly, correct?
In a world where I roll a twenty-sided dice 6,000 times, which is close to the number of setups we started with, it will land on a perfect twenty 5% of the time, or 300 times. Applying this to the fm, this means that for every mafia kill targeting a specific player will instantly collapse 300 worlds of that person being mafia. For me, that means there are already 900 worlds where I cannot be mafia.

Tl;dr - I agree with your consensus.
