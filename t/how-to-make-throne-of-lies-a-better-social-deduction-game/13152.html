NuclearBurrito November 12, 2017, 9:42pm #1

When I play social deduction games im looking to do 2 thing’s

A: Lie

B: Find liers

Now in the game the BD is doing B and the Unseen/NK is usually doing A. This is great and all but the best rounds involve doing both.

Remember ToS? One of the best roles in that game is the unrevealed mayor. Why? Because your a town that can legitimately fake claim and has reason to do so. You can claim your invest ad that X person should wisper claim to you. Or that your jailor and everyone should claim to you now to save time. Someone calls you out on it and you reveal.

Now simply having an easily confirmable class is not good enough. You also need to give the evils a reason to act differently in the face of a lie.

There is 1 example of this I can think of: let’s say your Good king and the sheriff wants you to out an Unseen for him. You could wisper that unseen something like “Yo. Evil king here! Who is the other Unseen?” And then out them all when/if he tells you.

The key here is that the evils didn’t know all of their Ally’s and can be tricked because of it.

Now while there are roles like the witch in ToS that isn’t enough. What you need is an Unseen/Cult class that doesn’t meet with them that can get information on them and others.

Every heard of the corrupt in FoL? They had a source that was NS, could get classes and visits and was critical to winning. That’s the kind of class I’m talking about. If you act as a class like that then you get more information out of evils.

Now I’m not suggesting we add the corrupt but what I am saying is that we need a scum Neutral investigative that can be faked by BD to gain the unseen/cults trust.

There is another kind of uncertainty however. A class that can change your objective without telling you would open up so meny door’s.

Example: (this probably isn’t balanced at all)

The Evangelical - BD special

Night ability: Make a player’s wincon change to yours. Does not notify them or change their class. Will fail if used on an unconvertible neutral. (1 use)

The Corrector - Unseen special

Night ability: The next Conversion will not notify the target and will not change their class or add the the maximum Unseen (1 use)

You can them your class and what you just did (even if you did nothing). The example I gave would not work on its own but something similar might.
