well, you tend to memorize anything you repeatedly use, whether thats for school or for focusing on a particular skill.
for instance, theres quite a few instances where ive figured out how to trigger a particular muscle without triggering the muscles that would normally go with it.
examples:

  • you know how when you yawn there can be that sorta static-like snow in your ear? i can do that on command in my right ear (cant figure out left for whatever reason. a lot of these are sided like that)
  • you know when you raise your eyebrows like in surprise, your face kinda pulls back, so it feels kinda tight? that too. visually, it moves my hair up a bit and my ears move a bit
  • raise one eyebrow (only the left)
  • smile with one half of my mouth (only the right side)
  • bend a bit of my ring finger so it makes basically a 90 degree angle, without bending the rest of my fingers (can kinda do it in both, right side is stronger tho)
  • cross eyes on command, cross one eye from straight ahead (none crossed to 1 crossed), cross 1 from already crossing (2 crossed to 1 crossed) and actively switch which eye is swapped (1 crossed to the other one crossed, without stopping at 0 or 2 crossed)
  • roll my eyes back into my head a bit
  • actively uncross my eyes, from a position where the thing im looking at is already in focus
  • wink in either eye, but also perpetually keep one eye half-closed

Its fun to practice them in my spare time.
