Shurian:

What is a complex number

numbers outside of the numberline

the numbers on the numberline are generally referred to as the real numbers, and complex numbers imaginary, but its a misleading name as complex numbers are still numbers, just as much as the reals, except their less used. i think. i havent actually been taught much, especially with the nomenclature, but ive picked up a few things

perhaps complex numbers there are referring to the numbers you get when you add real and imaginary numbers.

so, you may have heard of i. its a standin for the square root of -1. its basically the only imaginary number you’ll see (i havent heard of any others… at all. unless you count infinities).
so a complex number would be a+bi, where a and b are real numbers.
for instance, 2+3i.
this is useful, as it helps us represent coordinate pairs as a single number, that can be multiplied, added, squared, etc. (basically its a 2d vector) this is done by plotting these numbers on the complex plane, where the x-axis is the normal numberline, and the y-axis is the numberline, but as a coefficient of i
so 2+3i would plot the point (2,3) on the complex plane.
