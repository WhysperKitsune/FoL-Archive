An excerpt from the official history textbook of the Human Empire, Year Six.

Part 3: The Talion Trials – Continued

Despite the failure of the Martial Law policy, Talion continued to implement more restrictive measures in response to the Gold Rush Mission. For example, acting under orders from Alva’s Inferior Council, they placed all residents under house arrest for the duration of every night, and required all residents to publicly submit to a full-body search daily. Additionally, they dispatched a trained mind-reader to Talion.

The latter proved controversial even among Talion’s own residents. Fortunately, the foresight of the members of the Gold Rush Mission had led them to plan for such a possibility, and all of them had been equipped with imperceptibly small Brands of Mental Opacity, allowing them to resist such invasions of privacy. However, many of the elves living in Talion had no way to resist such measures.

The disputes came to a head when Kymil, an elven jeweler residing in Talion, announced that he would not submit to mindreading. His speech, given to the entire town, is the origin of the famous quote “My mind I guard more precious than my life.” The other residents of Talion, however, assumed that he was a human, and sought to punish him with death. As he fled Talion’s angry mob, Kymil ran to the top of the city’s watchtower and destroyed the Mystical Beacon at the top of the tower, which was used to allow visitors to Talion to locate the city. In doing so, he ensured that the mind-reader would not easily be able to complete their journey to Talion.

Comprehension Questions

  1. What additional restrictions were put in place in Talion after the initial failure of the Talion Trials? Name at least two.
  2. How did Talion’s residents react to the imposition of these restrictions?
  3. Name one indirect consequence of these restrictions.

Discussion Questions

  1. Some people have compared Talion’s mindreading procedure to the use of mindreading in Human courts. Why is this comparison faulty?
  2. The foresight of the members of the Gold Rush Mission allowed them to avoid any threat from mind-readers. What are some other ways that foresight might have helped them?
  3. What do you think about Kymil’s decision to destroy the Mystical Beacon of Talion?

End of Night 3 Flavor

An excerpt from the most popular history textbook of the Province of Alva, typically used for children between the ages of forty and fifty.

Chapter 4: The Gold Rush

Profile: Isylinor

Isylinor Cometchild (2453-2709) was born in Etolia in the Province of Alva. Although she was initially trained as an apprentice printer, she decided in 2579 to move to Talion and retrain as a sculptor. She was responsible for several innovations in magical item creation, most notably the discovery that sapphire inlays could be used as part of metal enchantments in order to increase the durability without compromising the strength of the magical effect.

During the Gold Rush Invasion, Isylinor played a prominent role in coordinating communications with other areas in the Province of Alva. She was able to use enchanted mirrors in order to supplement the other telepathic communication spells in-use. This allowed for more complex messages to be relayed. She was posthumously awarded the Medal of Resourcefulness, one of Alva’s highest honors, for her actions during the crisis.

Unfortunately, her levelheadedness made her a target for the invading humans, causing her to become the fourth elf to be murdered in the night. Every year, on the anniversary of her death, many people visit statue gardens to honor her.

3. How can you display those same traits in your own life? How can you use them to help your community?
