The Spy and The Mole (Social)

Class/Lore/Fanfic
Sheepish December 16, 2017, 2:36am #1

Here goes what is probably another failed attempt to suggest a social class.
NuclearBurrito did us all a huge favor when he listed old and possible new abilities for social classes, so it helped me try again since my last attempt failed.

The Spy (Blue Dragon Social)

Passive: Government Secrecy - Spy visits are hidden.

Day Ability 1: Private Matters (2 uses) - Whispers from and to you are hidden from the court.
Day Ability 2: I Spy with my Little Eyes (3 uses) - Whispers from and to the target are shown to you.

Night Ability 1: Eavesdrop (Unlimited uses) - Check if the target talked that night. You will not know who he talked to or what he said. Framed targets appear as having spoken. This ability triggers its answer as soon as the person speaks.
Night Ability 2: Burn After Reading (3 uses) - Prepare a message that will be sent at the beginning of the next morning to the target. Your name will not appear in the message. The target will receive something along the lines of “Spy: #7 claimed Sheriff to the king but spoke last night.” Only the target and the Spy will know about the existence of such a message. Activating this skill without choosing a target aims it at the Prince. The Spy will never be informed the Prince’s number, however.

Win Condition: Unseen/Cult loses

The Mole (Unseen Social)

Passive: Government Secrecy - Mole visits are hidden.

Day Ability 1: Private Matters (2 uses) - Whispers from and to you are hidden from the court.
Day Ability 2: Expose (3 uses) - Reveal to the Unseen who the Prince jails tonight.

Night Ability 1: Careless Whispers (2 uses) - The Unseen may whisper each other without the court knowing about it during the next day.
Night Ability 2: Noise Complaint (3 uses) - Silence a player until the next morning. If the target is neither in the dungeon nor linked with a psychic. This player will be silenced at the beginning of the next morning. This alternative effect lasts until the night.

Win Condition: BD loses

So, the Spy and the Mole are another shot at getting a new Social class in. They are entirely focused on communication mechanics. The Spy is a great tool against evil kings, just like the Noble once was. He’s kit revolves around finding people sending strange whispers and check them later that night. It’s important to remember that people speak to the Prince and the Prince to them during nighttime and the same applies to Psychics. This skill could even be triggered by a fool who decided to talk to himself at night to lure a Spy accusation the next morning. So I don’t think it’s OP. Noise Complaint is a direct social attack on Unseen/Cult’s face.
And then he gets converted! The Spy becomes a Mole and the Mole is a huge weapon against the Prince (if he wasn’t killed n1). He is that because he can silence a jailed player (Noise Complaint isn’t prevented on jailed players) and that directly leads to lack of information to the Prince and a possible execute that could get him to lose his ability to Execute altogether. Prince players would have to be extra careful in Spy games because of the chance that he may be converted.
One of the things I like the most about these classes is that they don’t depend on their alliances to win. All that matters to them is that their enemies lose. The NK can win, there could be a solo fool win or two alchemists winning by themselves. You win with them because your enemy lost.
Come on guys, it’s a nice idea! Give me that juicy feedback!!

Funny note: If you think about it, Spies can actually counter Moles if they are lucky enough, which is awesome! Also, two Spies could totally work together without anyone knowing about it.

Side note: As said by 3DT_CheesyKnives, this could also work as a Noble rework if we added Royal Blood to it.

Sheepish December 15, 2017, 8:18pm #3

  1. Framed People would appear as if they talked during the night, regardless of if they did. Eavesdrop works like this:
    The Spy targets player 13. If 13 SPEAKS that night, the Spy will be notified. If 13 doesn’t speak, the Spy will receive a message saying that his target’s room was silent.

  2. I think it’s important that the Mole has Noise Complaint at night so that he can use it with Expose. That’s almost the whole purpose of it. You find out who the Prince jailed and silence him. Swapping their places would make it so you randomly silence someone and then find out who the Prince jailed.

  3. I both agree and disagree with your feedback on Spy’s Noise Complaint. I’ll try to think of something.

  4. About the whisper reading, I feel like most of the boredom brought by it in the previous versions was because of a lack of purpose. Nobles would read countless whispers in the hopes of finding 3 saying “Hey, I’m MM, you are an evil king, right?”. 99.9% of the time that didn’t happen. So the Noble ended up just copying information to his logs until he was either dead or nominated the new king.
    Spy’s whisper reading has a purpose. You have the tools in your kit to actually confirm those you suspect. Step one, you see a sheriff claim. Step two, you find out that the sheriff is speaking at night. Step three, you ask who was jailed and linked by the psychic. Step four, you hang the mastermind. Something along those lines. You don’t have to rely on other people to actually do something like Noble did. Once you get converted, you forget about the whisper reading and focus on hiding whispers. Which is really unseen-friendly.

Thanks for always giving me your feedback! Like really!
