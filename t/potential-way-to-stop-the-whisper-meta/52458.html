meta gaming has never been a sanely enforcable rule… because when it comes down to it you won’t get agreements on it, and once you open that can of worms you get a huge mess.

That’s why even “everyone knows and hates it” cases they always solve it by fixing the mechanics that make it possible like in town of salem when jailors asked people to read their class card verbatim,

or in ToL where princes asked people to describe their room, it was solved by adding room pictures to the in game class info. When metagamers demanded people to copy/paste their “you occupied X” message, they made system messages unselectable etc…

A simple challange, if you made a topic called “what is meta gaming”. give say 10 examples. Things like “prince confirming what he is via something he said in jail”, “Psychic telepathically announcing he is psychic”, Demanding logs, asking to whisper classes etc…

You’ll really quickly find that almost nobody agrees with eachother on where the line is. Realizing that we can’t agree on a definition… is going to make it real obvious why they sure as heck aren’t going to want to try and enforce it… as the mod’s look over every report, there wouldn’t be a game going by where someone was reported for metagaming, and every punishment, there would be a 50/50 split of people saying “yes that’s deserved” “no this is nonsense moderation”.

If you want to talk a serious can of worms… metagaming moderation is absolutely the biggest nightmare imaginable.
