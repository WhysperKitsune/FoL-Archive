“Huzzah!” said one of the scientists when the new leader had finally been chosen. “Now that we’ve selected a leader, we can finally eradiate the conspiracy!” He ran his hand through his black hair.

“Of course there’s a conspiracy!” said the first scientist. “Do you think we just got dropped in an alternate reality by accident?”

“Accidents happen all the time!” said a third scientist. “The discovery of penicillin was an accident! For you to keep calling it a conspiracy, you must know something that we don’t.”

The first scientist closed his eyes, turned away, and unbuttoned his lab coat. As he shook off the coat, he revealed long red robes underneath his coat, with strange symbols emblazoned on them.

“What’s the costume for?” asked the intern. “Were you dressing up for a Halloween party, several centuries early?”

The first scientist smiled cruelly. “This universe is the realm of Corax!” he said. “All souls who pass away here become kindling on his altar, regardless of who they were devoted to in life. And by travelling back in time, I can give them all kinds of innovations – vaccinations, sanitation, electricity – so that there may be vastly more souls for him to consume.”

“I’m so confused by what your goals here are…” muttered another scientist. “But you’re responsible for our experiment going wrong?”

The scientist – Marluxion – sighed. “I want to ensure that as many humans can live in this universe as possible,” he said. “So that my god has more souls to utilize. It’s not that complicated.”

“Well, we’ll be sending you to your god!” said the intern, running at him and hitting him on the head with a thick binder. Marluxion collapsed to the ground, never to wake again.

“What brings you up here so late?” asked Windward, turning to greet Nightingale as she reached the top of the bell tower.

Nightingale’s eyes widened. “It’s been changing me too,” she said. She drew her hands apart, and ice crystals sparkled between the two of them. “I didn’t want to believe it at first. No one expects scientists to believe in magic. But every experiment I’ve conducted has come back with the same result.”

“It’s been the same for us,” said PKR. “Just after sundown, I felt an overwhelming impulse to swear fealty to Windward. I don’t know what this dimension is doing to my mind, but it’s clearly something.”

Windward bit her lip. “I have a hypothesis,” she said. “Not a certain one, but I think we should consider it. Marluxion talked about how our souls would feed his god, did he not? Well, if you were a god, would you rather have the soul of an ordinary person, or a wizard?”

As Windward spoke, the ground began to shake. The three of them tried to hold on as best they could to the walls, but they couldn’t manage to avoid being flung from the top of the tower to the ground. The bell rang out three times – bong, bong, bong – and was silent, leaving no sign of the conversation but three broken corpses.
