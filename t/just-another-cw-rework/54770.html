Right now fool has very little to explain a prevented visit. Claiming you were jailed is easily counter-claimed and just invites a jail the next night. Claiming you are a merc target doesnâ€™t go very far if nobody is going to claim to be your merc (and TB can only go so far there).

This CW design gives MMs/NKs a BD claim to explain death immunity, gives Poss/Sorc an easier BD claim to explain occ immunity, and gives all evils a claim that is not readily disputable. Thus, I expect this CW would be a popular scum claim. As with knight, the meta response may be to hang CW claims indiscriminately. To counteract that, I wanted to give CW something that would attract fools. The passive does exactly that, giving the fool license to use his hide night ability without being instantly pegged as a fool.

Additionally, this CW has the empower tool that would make it a valuable support BD. Empower can make prince death immune, which will be even more important if the meta continues toward prince outting early. The CW can also protect a Hunter who is going to wolf, assist butlers/drunks with locking down Poss/MM, prevent cult from swapping healers off bleeding/valuable targets, prevent conversion of mystics/nobles, and improve investigative results.

This CW will be more interesting to play because there are many different strategies and playstyles. Siphon can keep you alive at night, but at the expense of not really developing verifiable logs. Alternatively, the passive allows you to instead focus on boosting key BD through Empower. More, empower provides different boosts to different classes, thus making the CW versatile and flexible. But siphon is not overpowered. A CW could be death immune or conversion immune, but not both. And a CW who is occâ€™d on a siphon night will be completely vulnerable the next night.

At the same time, empower creates more space by blurring results. A butler who is empowered will not know whether his target was occ immune. A Knight or hunter that is alch bombed may be accused of being the NK the next day.
