Possessed flavor

The young squire, barely seven years old but bright and eager, had always admired the older man, and although he was too young to formally start his training in the service of the crown, he had eagerly followed the man around, badgering him with incessant questions about battles he had fought in, jousts he had triumphed in, innocents he had saved. There were those who said that the shadow of the conspiracy had touched the hearts of everyone, and although the Squire wasn’t sure what that meant, the man was unaffected; when he saw the squire, he would tell him of the importance of honor, of giving your life before letting an innocent die, and of ensuring that you never harm one who is not your enemy.

So it came to be, that morning, that the squire, intending to figure out why the man was late for training, instead tripped over him lying in the hallway, his sword by his side.

“Wake up,” said the squire, shaking him. “Wake up!”

The squire recalled the man’s stories of death, and looking more closely, he realized the truth. The Knight, his hero, was gone.
