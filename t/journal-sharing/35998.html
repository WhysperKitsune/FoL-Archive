Journal sharing?

Feedback/Suggestions
FidelCastro January 11, 2018, 7:48am #1

Journal sharing can be a pain in the butt as it is. It often takes more than one copy and paste to get everything, and even then when the log is concise. Some roles can have really long logs-- obs and sheriff in particular, but also psychic and noble if they’re recording info from dead or logs.

Could we not just have an option to share a link to our journal, in the same manner the noble can now see saved journals? Maybe tied to a command like /j?

Aquillion January 11, 2018, 8:55am #2

I’m unsure about this. I do usually like suggestions that streamline the interface and mechanics, but this would have a lot of side-effects.

In particular, this would make writing and maintaining fake journals a bit more of a pain in the ass. You would have to keep an actual, singular fake journal in your journal; you wouldn’t really have time to copy-paste in everything from the notepad (and there isn’t even enough space there to completely fill out both pages of the journal, so having a one-page journal would become a tell.)

I would strongly oppose this, if it weren’t for the noble change that I already oppose. I really don’t like snoop on BD, it really cuts down on flexibility in claiming. Normally the rule of thumb for evil is to keep your claims liquid and changable until forced to claim when it becomes concrete. People seeing your actual journal when alive basically means you may have already claimed without knowing it, and thus need to stick to that story.

Say if you are mastermind, and you get called out by a princess as special/investigative, having a sheriff or princess journal is what you want, but if a drunk/butler calls you out, you’d need a hunter/drunk/butler journal ready, so having both USED to be the smart play.

Adding in a 1 button show logs button, would add extra work if badguys are doing what used to be the smart way to play, which is keeping a liquid claim, multiple claims ready etc… making a 1 button to show the logs hurts that playstyle as the added delay to edit the logs and take the right one could be proof of guilt when good guys would just have a single button (normally the difference in human reaction times and computer speeds make judging that difficult, but if it’s a single button there’s no excuse for a delay)
