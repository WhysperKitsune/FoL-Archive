orangeandblack5 October 7, 2017, 4:36pm #25

Split currencies can be done REALLY well. Just look at Rainbow Six: Siege.

All of these purchases are cosmetic-only. Most things can be bought for Renown, the earnable currency. Some things require R6 Credits, which are purchased with real money in packs and given to season pass buyers, including boosters to gain more Renown and some exclusive cosmetic items. However, these items can also be earned through a loot-box system called Alpha Packs (which currently has no way to purchase them with real money, only with Renown), along with rare “Legendary” items which can only be found in these Alpha Packs (which, again, you cannot buy with real money). Then there are also some things purchasable as “Digital Content”, which are essentially tiny cosmetic DLC packs with several items (usually with a theme, such as gemstones, other Ubisoft games, or packs to support the game’s Pro Leage) that you can only purchase directly with money instead of having to buy a currency pack to purchase it.

Long story short, things can be priced well enough to be reasonably earn-able in-game, still get money for Ubisoft, and everybody is happy.
