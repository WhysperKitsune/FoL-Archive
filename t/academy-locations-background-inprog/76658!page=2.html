Orbal Revolution

Just 50 years ago, Contrast was founded by Gywn Contrast with his group of mechanics. They spearheaded the Orbal Revolution, making usage of quartz orbs to power our everyday lives. An explosive age where modernization occured, they quickly raked in money.

The Great Divide

In the year 1575, the union Lineara split to become Adiart, Telias and Agravain respectively. Through the next 2 centuries, wars often broke out between the two nations Adiart and Telias. It was only in 1782 when the two nations made peace with one another, through the Transcontinental Ceasefire Agreement. Even so, the two nations have been at odds with one another, the conflict still brews even till today.

Northern Forest

Many know of the Forest. But they never set foot in it. There is a rumor. Once you get in, you never get out.

Jaegar Corps

These mercenaries corporations are all about the money. They do the job, no matter how dirty. The opposite of the humanitarian Bracers. Gold, is all that speaks for them.

Ousting of the Bracers

In recent years, the provincial armies have ousted the Bracer guilds one by one from the country, forcing Bracers to leave the country or change professions.
The official reason is that they were overarching their rights on the people, infringing on the noble’s territory. Yet, many say that they were afraid of bracers taking over the trust of the people, thus removing them.
There is only one Bracer guild left, residing in Tempor.

Principal Vandyke Ferlias

A member of the Ferlias family, a commoner whose ancestors fought with the first Emperor Adiart to unite the western lands. He is known for being one of the strongest commanders in Adiart. After retiring from service as the High Commander of the Imperial Armies, he made his way to education. A strong advocate for peace, he trains the next generation for his homeland’s protection.

He is said to be able to bring a tank down in a single blow, but that was while he was in his prime. Now? He could be just a feeble old man.

Josephine Rogner

A teacher focusing on the political side of education. She teaches and oversees each student’s training. She has her pride as a noble, yet she does things as she sees fit. A black sheep in her family, her father and her are not on good terms.

Thomas Rieveldt

A history teacher. Extremely carefree, often bringing down the house with his ramblings of history. Speaks often of history and history. Too much history. People doubt he even has some kind of history.

Leonardo Reinhardt

A military lieutenant in the Imperial Army 2nd Armored Division. He is a serious person, often butting heads with one particular teacher, for her unusual personality as an instructor. He teaches the military tactics class in the school.

Sara Valestein

A teacher who oversees the combat training of each student. She is known to be flippant, relaxed and easy-going.

Beatrice Le Louche

A nurse that was a combat nurse in her service. She is adept at treating wounds, as she had her fair share of stories to tell. She is the kind and caring wife of the principal Vandyke.

Herschel Leif

This kind and gentle teacher can’t hurt a fly! She is the soft-handed teacher that teaches art classes here in this academy. Not only that, she is an adept at nursing as well!

Harst Boldrun

This is the vice-president of the school, harsh and militaristic. He prides himself as a noble and is convinced that all nobles are better than commoners. He is strict to all, meting out punishments to those that do not follow school regulations. His sharp tongue has left many in tears.

The School Buildings.

There are a number of buildings to note of.

Main School Building.

Central.
The Infirmary, Teacher’s Lounge, Principal’s Office and Conference Room are on the right of the first floor. Second Year classrooms are on the left. Both ways have a staircase that leads to the second floor. The building is shaped like a Ushape.
On the second floor, we have Club Rooms on the right and First Year Classrooms on the left. And the third floor is a roof, allowing anyone to look over the place itself.
Club rooms include the Wind Orchestra Room, Art Room, Home Economics Room (Cooking Club) and the Computer Room (usually locked up).

Student Union

East of the school building.
The canteen is on the first floor. Together with a weapons shop, that coincidentally sells quartz as well, you won’t be missing much from this shop. On the second floor, you’ll find more club rooms and the student council room at the end of the hallway. And on the third floor, we have a quiet study session area.
Club rooms include… Lower and Upper chess clubs. Literature Club, Photography Club and Fishing Club.

The Library

Straight East of the entrance.
Southwest of the building.
Study here, with much information about the world’s history, one can never go wrong here.
Library is open.

The Auditorium

Southwest of the school building.
Usually locked up when not in use, this is for students to practice their fine arts.
Locked.

The Gymnasium.

North of the school building.
Sporting the club room for the Fencing Club, as well as a swimming pool, this place can be popular at times. Also, on the second floor are several storerooms for the Gardening Club and the clubs forementioned. The swimming club is having their meet now.

The Garden

North of the school building.
A haven for the Gardening Club, this place is where the flowers grow. There is a small pond for fishing enthusiasts to fish on. This is besides the Gymnasium and north of the school building. The Gardening Club does not seem to have a meeting for today.

The Fields

West of the School Building.
A place where students train in the aspect of fighting, where the Riding and Lacrosse clubs belong.
Current Status: Riding Club and Lacrosse Clubs are up.

Engineering Building.

Northeast of the School Building. Houses the Engineering Club. Open.
